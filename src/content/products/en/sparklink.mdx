With SparkLink, Sparkium eLearning content becomes usable in real-world systems.
It acts as the bridge between structured content and learning management systems (LMS) such as Moodle, enabling seamless synchronization, updates, and distribution without manual rework.

## How does Sparklink work?
SparkLink takes structured Sparkium eLearning materials and turns it into live courses in your LMS. It handles validation, coordination, and delivery for you.

1. Process eLearning materials: 
Sparklink takes and validates Sparkium content for LMS delivery
1. Sync and launch on LMS: 
Upload content to LMS platforms like Moodle, publish your course and make it accessible to your students!

## SparkLink, what else?

Most eLearning content still ends up being manually uploaded, updated, and maintained inside LMS platforms.  SparkLink introduces a different approach: automated delivery from structured content into real systems.

| Comparison                    | Manual LMS Upload Workflow               | SCORM / Package Uploads              | xAPI-based Integrations              | SparkLink                                                                 |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Content Input**            | 🔴 Files or courses created inside LMS      | 🔴 Pre-packaged SCORM files             | 🔴 Event-based data streams            | 🟢 Structured Sparkium content (text + file system)                         |
| **Update Process**           | 🔴 Manual re-edit inside LMS                | 🔴 Re-export and re-upload packages     | 🔴 Requires system-side integration     | 🟢 Update source content once and sync automatically                           |
| **Workflow Complexity**      | 🔴 High (LMS-dependent editing)             | 🟡 Medium (packaging required)          | 🔴 High (technical integration needed)  | 🟢 Low (With a few clicks)                                 |
| **Technical Barrier**        | 🟡 Low to start, high to maintain           | 🟡 Medium                               | 🔴 High                                 | 🟢 Low                                                   |
